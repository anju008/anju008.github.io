Growth insights from eCommerce data

A practical analysis focused on what to fix first: channel dependency, product revenue patterns, and lifecycle leverage.

Project summary

Tools: BigQuery (SQL) · Looker Studio · thelook_ecommerce public dataset

Project type: Growth analytics · Channel performance · Product intelligence

The problem

Revenue reporting alone doesn’t tell you where to invest next. Without channel + product visibility, teams keep spending on the loudest acquisition lever instead of the most efficient one.

What I did

I queried and joined orders, users, products, and order items in BigQuery, then built a Looker Studio dashboard to make trends and decisions immediately visible.

The three questions:

  1. Which channels drive the most revenue—and is that healthy?
  2. Which products generate the most money—and why?
  3. What does customer spending behavior look like across segments?

Insights that matter

  • Search dominates while AOV stays similar across channels → opportunity is scaling underused channels, not “better customers.”
  • Email is underweighted → likely highest ROI lever with lifecycle automation and segmentation.
  • Product winners split into two patterns → premium margin protection + mid-range paid scaling.

What I’d do next

  1. Lifecycle flows to grow Email’s contribution without more ad spend.
  2. Paid scaling plan around mid-range high-volume winners.
  3. Experiment backlog for CRO + channel expansion (Display, affiliates, partnerships).
