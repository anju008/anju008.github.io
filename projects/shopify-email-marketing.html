How I approach luxury email marketing differently

Luxury email marketing is not about saying more. It is about having the confidence to say less.

Standard brands use urgency, repetition, and offers to push decisions. Premium brands earn trust by staying composed — they write shorter copy, leave more white space, and never chase the customer.

  • No countdown timers or stock scarcity tactics
  • No discount in the first cart abandonment email — patience signals confidence
  • One clear action per email, never two competing CTAs
  • Copy that sounds like a person wrote it, not a marketing team
  • Design that gives the content room to breathe
The best luxury email makes the reader feel that the brand barely needed to send it — and that is exactly why she opens the next one.

Each flow below was mapped in Miro before being built in Klaviyo. A/B test hypotheses and KPI targets are embedded directly into the flow diagrams.

Welcome Flow

Goal: Make a strong first impression and establish brand trust before any product push. The Welcome email does not try to sell anything. It introduces the brand — who Gapianne is, what they believe in, and what sets their approach to intimate wellness apart. No discount code, no product carousel, no urgency. A premium brand earns attention before asking for anything.

Cart Abandonment Flow

Trigger: Customer adds a product to cart and begins checkout but does not complete the purchase.
Goal: Bring the customer back to complete her order, without pressure, without tactics. The Cart Abandonment email is minimal by design: one clean product image, two short sentences of copy, and one button — "Return to your cart." No countdown timer, no "Only 2 left in stock," and no discount offered. Urgency and scarcity are mass-market tactics. They work by creating anxiety, and anxiety is the opposite of how a premium brand should make its customers feel.

Post Purchase Flow

Trigger: Customer completes a purchase.
Goal: Reassure the customer, confirm her order, and make her feel her decision was the right one. The Post Purchase email is a single warm confirmation. After a purchase, the customer needs reassurance, not a cross-sell. Sending a product recommendation immediately after checkout feels transactional and cheap. The post-purchase moment is about cementing trust and loyalty.

Email Drafts Using Klaviyo

I designed these emails to support a premium brand experience by keeping the messaging calm, minimal, and intentional at every step. The welcome email focuses on brand introduction rather than selling, so the first interaction feels warm and editorial instead of promotional. The cart abandonment email uses one clean product image, short copy, and a single CTA to gently remind the customer without pressure, urgency, or discount tactics that can feel too aggressive for a premium brand. The post-purchase email is written as a simple confirmation and thank-you message, because after someone buys, the brand should reassure them and strengthen trust rather than immediately try to sell again. Across all emails, I kept the layout airy, the copy restrained, and the tone composed so the brand feels thoughtful, confident, and high-end.
