E-Commerce Performance Dashboard

A BigQuery + Looker Studio build designed to answer the questions that actually change spend: channel health, product concentration, and retention opportunities.

Project summary

Tools: BigQuery (SQL) · Looker Studio · thelook_ecommerce public dataset

Project type: Growth analytics · Channel performance · Product intelligence

The problem

Most E-commerce businesses track revenue, but few know where it comes from, which products are driving it, and which channels are underperforming relative to their potential. Without that visibility, budget follows noise—not efficiency.

What I did

Using Google’s public thelook_ecommerce dataset (a fictitious clothing retailer with 21,000+ completed orders), I wrote SQL queries in BigQuery across orders, users, products, and order items, then built a Looker Studio dashboard to visualize the results.

The three questions:

  1. Which channels drive the most revenue—and is that healthy?
  2. Which products generate the most money—and why?
  3. What does customer spending behavior look like across segments?

Key insights (high signal)

  • Search drives ~73% of revenue, but AOV is similar across channels (\($59–62\)). The gap is volume, not customer quality.
  • Email is under-leveraged (7% of orders), signaling a large owned-revenue opportunity with lifecycle automation.
  • Category concentration risk: outerwear & coats dominate top revenue products—great for margin, risky for dependence.

What I’d do next (if this were live)

  1. Fix the Email channel: build 5 lifecycle flows (welcome, browse abandon, cart abandon, post-purchase, winback).
  2. Scale mid-range winners on paid: better ROAS potential than premium low sell-through items.
  3. Investigate Display: highest AOV but lowest volume—if CPA is reasonable, it can scale.
